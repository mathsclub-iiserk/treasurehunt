Q4: Stumble
The raging storm won't die down. It has been going on for three days at a stretch. Many crewmates have been plunged into the sea, or have died in the sudden fire that broke out on deck. You are at your wits end, as you sit in your cabin, waiting for the end. You recall that one day you were dead drunk, and went out for a stroll at night. If only you had never returned ...
A drunkard walks out of home at night. The probability that he takes a step away from home is $p > 1/2$, and the probability that he takes a step towards home is $1 - p$. Once he is at home, he can only step out of it. Given the night is infinitely long, how many times does the drunkard reach home?
If it you have a finite answer, write that number. Otherwise, write infinity.

Q9: Build
You didn't think that Calvin gets any credit for an obviously flawed argument. You move forward trying to look for more clues and arrive at a narrow passageway going up to a waterfall. Along the wall, you see a familiar problem stated.
There is a circle $\Gamma$ whose diameter is labelled $D$. A point $P$ can be either on $\Gamma$, inside $\Gamma$, or outside $\Gamma$.
In how many of the three conditions can you draw a perpendicular from $P$ to $D$ with just a straight-edge and a ruler?

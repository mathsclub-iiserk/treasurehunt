Q8: Positivity
Those are some crazy rocks over yonder. They lie in ascending order of size, and each of their heights seem to have been subject to a very specific scaling. If you go down the rocky ledge,you can see a similar arrangement, but the scaling is exact in reverse order. What if you stack them on top of one another? Is this the work of some gruesome alien life that shall open a window to Edelweiss or Jotunheim?
The king gives you a polynomial of finite degree and positive integer coefficients, denoted by $p(x)$. He imposes the law that $$ p\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)\geq \frac{1}{p(x)} $$ for every $x > 0$. However, great thinkers are riddled with the precise conditions required to guarantee the same. Calvin crashes into this great convention and shouts out, “If the law holds when the variable is unity, then it holds for all that we need!" and looks at Hobbes with glee. Can the great thinkers give credit to Calvin?
Write yes if you agree with Calvin, no if not.
